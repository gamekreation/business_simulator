BUSINESS EMPIRE PROTOTYPE DEVELOPMENT SUMMARY
=============================================
Version Roadmap: V0.1 -> V0.2 -> V0.3
OS Environment: Windows Next.js App Router (Turbopack)

This document traces the complete design decisions, feature implementations, and bugs resolved throughout the pair-programming development of the Business Empire simulation game.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. VERSION 0.1: THE FOUNDATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The initial session created a single-player business manager played on an interactive 10x10 RTS-style grid.

Core Features:
* Grid Map: A coordinate mapping grid where players can buy and place infrastructure.
* Infrastructure Catalog: Basic extractors (Farms, Mines) and production factories.
* Simulation Engine: Runs every 1 second, updating cash flows, processing raw materials into goods, and charging building maintenance.
* Boardroom Mergers: Simple recipe checks that took adjacent basic buildings and merged them into Large Company HQs for massive passive revenue.
* Cloud Autosaves: Fully integrated Supabase client mapping grid states to local storage and remote PostgreSQL servers.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. VERSION 0.2: DEEP ECONOMIC STRATEGY (THE EXPANSION)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The goal of V0.2 was to transform the game from an idle builder into a strategic management simulator with authentic bottlenecks, complex production chains, and multi-currency RTS construction.

Centralized Data Configurations:
* Created src/buildings/buildingConfig.ts to completely decouple game properties from the simulation code.
* Declared a comprehensive Resource Schema defining volume (VU) footprints and base market prices.
* Created a data-driven Factory Recipe Registry mapping exact raw input and output units.

Specialized Business Categories:
* Retail Shops: Furniture, Food, Clothing, Medical, Grocery, and Electronics stores.
* Service Businesses: Interior Design, Architecture, Consulting, Auto Garages, and Hotels.
* Business Progression: Allowed upgrading placed shops and offices through multiple tiers (Shop/Office -> Showroom/Firm -> Dealership/Corporate).

Natural Resource Grid Overlay:
* Visual nodes placed on the map to define raw materials.
* Extractor placement rules: Mines must be built on matching deposit nodes. Farms must be placed on Fertile Land.

Volume-Based Warehouse Limits:
* Warehouse storage cap calculated by total cumulative item volume (VU), rather than individual unit counts.
* Production halts when warehouse reaches 100% capacity, forcing logistical coordination.

Logistics Cargo Fleet:
* Introduced a cargo fleet dashboard to purchase transport vehicles (Pickups, Vans, Medium/Heavy Trucks).
* Active cargo trucks apply global delivery speed buffs, accelerating retail sell intervals.

RTS Multi-Currency Construction:
* Replaced simple cash purchases with physical resource costs.
* Placing and upgrading buildings now requires Money, Iron Ore, Limestone, and Mortar.
* Added Mortar Mixing recipe (2 Limestone -> 1 Mortar).
* Implemented a top RTS-style resource HUD bar displaying Cash, Iron, Limestone, and Mortar.
* Setup bootstrapper to grant 20 Iron Ore, 20 Limestone, and 10 Mortar on new games and load-healing to prevent starting deadlocks.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. VERSION 0.3: LOCAL SILOS, COMMITTED CROP CYCLES & GLOBAL IMPORTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The V0.3 update introduced authentic physical loops, local storage bottlenecks, active agricultural management, and strategic trade-offs through imports.

Lumber Milling & Forest Resource Nodes:
* Wood & Forest nodes: Added Forest deposit nodes to the map layout.
* Lumber Mill: A new extractor built exclusively on Forest nodes to produce Wood.
* Changed Furniture factory recipe to require 2 Wood (instead of Steel & Fabric), creating a natural timber production chain.

Local Storage Silo Bottlenecks:
* Decoupled raw extraction outputs from the global warehouse: extractors and farms now deposit harvested items into their own building-local silos (up to 100 units * building level).
* If a local silo fills completely, extraction/farming automatically halts.

Dispatch-Based Logistics Shipment System:
* Automatic background hauling is replaced with a dispatch system.
* Extractors and farms accumulate harvested goods in their local silos.
* Players select a building and click "📤 Dispatch Shipment" to snapshot the current silo stock and transfer it into an active transit queue.
* The local silo is immediately emptied, allowing it to start gathering new production without interrupting the active transit.
* Each building can only have one active shipment in transit at a time.
* Logistics fleet vehicles haul resources only from these active shipments in discrete truckloads to the global warehouse.

Factory Target Quotas:
* Added production limit options to the Property Inspector for factories.
* Players can select a target quota (Infinite, 50, 100, 250, 500 units max in warehouse). When the quota is reached, the factory pauses operations and goes into low-wear mode.

Committed Agricultural Cycles:
* Farms no longer produce crops passively. Instead, players click on a farm on the grid and must choose between:
  - 🌾 Grow Food (Crops)
  - 🌱 Grow Cotton (raw Cotton material for Fabric)
* Grow cycles take 60 seconds. Crop choices are locked during active growth, creating a strategic planning commitment. Completed cycles yield 124 crop units directly to the farm's local silo.

Global Material Import Terminal:
* Added a materials import deck inside the Dashboard.
* Players can purchase 50 units of any raw natural resource (Iron, Coal, Lime, Oil, Wood, Cotton, Mortar) instantly at a premium price.
* Import orders queue incoming cargo deliveries with a 15-second timer countdown, representing a strategic tradeoff between high-cost quick imports and long-term self-built extraction.

Structural Integrity Decay (Revamped Maintenance):
* Maintenance is no longer charged as a continuous cash leak.
* Integrity decay scales with building work status:
  - Working buildings (actively producing, extracting, hauling, or selling) experience a **low wear rate** (-0.12% per tick) due to active maintenance.
  - Idle/Stopped buildings (due to critical breakdowns, full silos, lack of inputs, or quotas reached) experience a **high neglect rate** (-0.35% per tick).
* At 0% integrity, the building experiences a structural breakdown: all extraction, manufacturing, retail sales, or service revenues are paused.
* Players can pay a one-time repair fee (calculated based on structural base cost and current integrity status) to restore building health to 100 and resume operations immediately.

RTS Wood Construction Costs:
* Wood is now a core construction resource. Constructing or upgrading buildings now requires Money, Iron, Limestone, Mortar, and Wood.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3b. VERSION 0.4 CORE FEATURES (INDUSTRIAL DEPARTMENTS & CORPORATE MERGERS)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Industry-Specific Departments:
* Every building category (Extractors, Factories, Retail, Services) has 3 custom departments mapping real-world business success factors:
  - *Extractors*: Extraction Efficiency, Silo Capacity, Safety & Maintenance.
  - *Factories*: Automation & Robotics, Material Science, Storage Logistics.
  - *Retail* (Shops, Gas Stations): Visual Merchandising Display, Branding & Premium Placement, Store Floor Expansion.
  - *Services*: Executive Expertise, Client Relations, Cost Accounting.
* Upgraded building levels grant 1 Department Point (DP) per level. Players allocate these points (up to level 5 max per department) to unlock custom strategic buffs.

Retail Gas Stations:
* Added a new constructible Retail building: **Gas Station** (Costs $450, 15 Iron, 10 Lime, 5 Mortar, 25 Wood).
* Gas Stations sell refined Fuel from your global warehouse directly to consumers, allowing you to establish a fuel retail pipeline from the start of the game.

Locked Factory Specializations:
* Factories are now permanently specialized upon placement.
* Clicking to place a factory opens a popup selection modal prompting the player to choose a permanent recipe (e.g. Food Processing, Steel Smelting, Furniture Crafting). Once chosen, this recipe is locked, and the dropdown selector is removed from the Property Inspector.

Factory Recipe Merger Safety Constraints:
* Mergers now verify the specific recipe of factories.
* For example, merging an Electricity Board HQ strictly checks for a Small Factory specialized in Coal Power Generation, ensuring your separate Furniture factories are protected and never accidentally consumed.

Progression Locks:
* Advancing business tiers is locked behind building levels and department milestones:
  - *Showroom (Tier 2)*: Requires Building Level 10 + Store Expansion Department Level 3.
  - *Dealership (Tier 3)*: Requires Building Level 25 + Store Expansion Department Level 5.

Merger Quality Score Inheritance:
* Merging component buildings into a Corporate HQ calculates a Brand Quality Score (average level score).
* HQ passive revenues are scaled by this quality score (0% to 100%). Rushing merges with level 1 shops creates weak corporations, whereas maxing them out yields massive profits.

Corporate Restructuring:
* Players can pay $1,000 cash to hire consultants to reset allocated department points back to the unallocated pool.

Decay Freezing for Paused Operations:
* Extractor mines freeze decay wear completely (0% wear) if their local storage silos are full.
* Factories freeze decay wear completely (0% wear) if their target production quotas are reached or if the global warehouse is too full to store their output.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3c. VERSION 0.5 INFRASTRUCTURE FEATURES (BUILDER CREWS & ROAD NETWORKS)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Construction Crews & Queue timers:
* Placed buildings and triggered upgrades are no longer instant. They are enqueued in an active construction queue.
* The player starts with 2 Builder Crews. Each active build/upgrade project occupies 1 crew.
* If all crews are busy, new building placements and level upgrades are blocked until a crew becomes free.
* RTS Construction Progress widgets (with countdown timers and dynamic visual progress bars) render directly over grid tiles.
* Buildings skip all operations (resource extraction, input consumption, manufacturing, sales, decay, etc.) while under construction or upgrading.

Transit Road Networks:
* Placed via the Construction catalog. Cost: $10, 1 Limestone, 1 Wood per road tile.
* Instantly constructed on 1x1 tiles. Displayed with a custom dark-asphalt texture and center dividing lanes.
* Each road tile built provides a global logistical hauling speed boost (+0.5% transport speed per tile) to speed up deliveries.

Map Resizing & Visual Optimization:
* Reduced scrolling grid panel to a clean, fit-to-screen **20 x 20** viewport (400 coordinates total).
* Cells automatically scale responsively (`w-full aspect-square`) to fit any device monitor size without scrolling.
* Stripped text overlays inside tiny cells to prevent layout distortions.
* Added native hover tooltips (`title`) describing building level, coordinates, roads, or natural resource node types.
* Pre-populated deposit nodes (Iron, Coal, Lime, Oil, Forests, Fertile Land) procedurally in cluster distributions.

Hydration Optimization & Starting Balances:
* Added a client-side mounting check (`isMounted`) that delays JSX rendering until local storage loads, removing startup layouts flickering.
* Adjusted new game parameters: starts with **$5,000**, **70 Iron Ore**, **70 Limestone**, **70 Mortar**, **70 Wood**, and **0 Cotton**.
* Set initial base warehouse capacity to **1,600** volume units.

4. SIGNIFICANT BUG FIXES & STABILITY RESOLUTIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* React StrictMode Double-Increments:
  - Issue: Upgrades and level-ups jumped from level 1 to level 3 in React dev mode.
  - Resolution: Refactored handleUpgradeBuilding, handleProgressTier, and handleUpgradeVehicleStat to use 100% pure, immutable mapping arrays (.map) rather than mutating nested state references directly.
* Duplicate Company Keys:
  - Issue: StrictMode double-execution caused key collision crashes (e.g. company-1783455916377).
  - Resolution: Added key deduplication sanitation filters inside data loaders on load-mount.
* Nested Config Mutation in Mergers:
  - Issue: Merger executions mutated global config limits directly.
  - Resolution: Deep-cloned merger requirements counts dynamically during evaluations.
* Company HQ boardroom upgrades:
  - Issue: The company boardroom tab was replaced in V0.2, leaving HQs un-upgradable.
  - Resolution: Added Corporate HQ Level and department skill controls directly inside the Property Inspector.
* Road Placement TypeError:
  - Issue: Attempting to place roads threw an undefined lookup error on `placingType.name` since roads are not in `BUILDING_CONFIGS`.
  - Resolution: Implemented safe checks for `placingType === "road"` fallback strings.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. VERSION 0.6: REFINED RECIPES, PROGRESSION AGE & SKILL TREE REDESIGN
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Industrial Progression Ages:
* Foundation Age: Natural resources available are Stone, Wood, Iron Ore, and Fertile Land. Cement is imported from the market; players cannot manufacture Cement. Primary construction materials are Iron, Stone, Wood, and Mortar.
* Industrial Age: Limestone, Coal, and Oil are unlocked, enabling Cement factories to manufacture Cement locally (Limestone + Coal -> Cement).

Factory Size-Locked Industries:
* Factory upgrades no longer expand recipe lists; instead, factory size dictates the available industry categories:
  - Small Factory: Consumer Goods (Food, Grocery Goods, Clothes, Furniture, Mortar).
  - Medium Factory: Heavy Industry (Cement, Steel, Fuel, Chemicals).
  - Large Factory: Advanced Manufacturing (Electronics, Medicine, Vehicles).
* Specialization is permanent upon selection. Upgrading factory level increases capacity metrics (internal storage, worker capacity, production queue) rather than unlocking recipes.

Corporate Mergers & HQ Construction:
* Corporate mergers now require physical construction resources (Money, Iron, Stone, Wood, Mortar) matching the HQ building configuration, simulating the cost of building a massive facility.

Modular Skill Tree Redesign:
* Small Businesses & Factories: Maximum level is set to 30, gaining 1 skill point per level. Contains exactly 5 industry-specific skills, each capping at level 5.
* Promotion Requirements: Advancing business tiers is locked behind:
  - Shop -> Showroom (Tier 2): Level 15 + Required Expansion-related Skill Level 3.
  - Showroom -> Dealership (Tier 3): Level 30 + Required Expansion-related Skill Level 5.
* Resource Mines & Quarries: Contains 3 universal skills (Extraction Technology, Heavy Machinery, and Storage Infrastructure) along with 2 unique skills based on the mined deposit (e.g. Conductivity Grade for Copper Mine).
* Corporate Mergers: Have 7 skills: 4 Universal Corporate Skills (Corporate Finance, Brand Management, Supply Chain Management, Business Expansion) along with 3 Industry-Specific Corporate Skills.
