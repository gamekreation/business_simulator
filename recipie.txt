💡 Suggestion for Antigravity

Tell Antigravity to make this 100% data-driven.

Instead of hardcoding factory recipes, create a configuration like:

Factory {
  name
  inputs[]
  outputs[]
  productionTime
  batchSize
  requiredFactorySize
  unlockedAge
}

Then adding a new factory later (for example, a Glass Factory or Plastic Factory) becomes just adding a new row of data instead of rewriting simulation logic. That will save a huge amount of development time as your game grows.

Progression System

Every business follows the same progression:

Maximum Level: 30
1 Skill Point per Level
Each Skill: Max Level 5
Total Skill Levels: 25 (leaving 5 flexible points for player choice)

Promotion requirements:

Business Tier	Requirement
Shop → Showroom	Business Level 15 + Required Expansion Skill Level 3
Showroom → Dealership	Business Level 30 + Required Expansion Skill Level 5

This creates meaningful choices. Players can't simply dump every point into profit—they must invest in expansion-related skills to unlock the next tier, while still deciding how much to specialize in marketing, production, efficiency, or customer experience. That balance makes each business feel different and encourages experimentation without permanently punishing mistakes (especially if you later allow skill resets for gems or in-game currency).
